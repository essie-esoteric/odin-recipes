Meatloaf

I grew up not liking meatloaf, but as it turns out, this is because the meatloaf I had growing up was bad. I'm pretty sure it was just straight up meat + ketchup + bread...

Anyways, I finally decided trying to make it on my own but butter and I was looking at some recipes and found a couple that had different attributes I liked and decided to combine them with good results!

This meatloaf has so much flavor, I really love it. It's pretty easy to throw together and it's such an easy thing to make a lot of and reheat for left-overs. Here is what you need:

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs ground beef, (preferably 85% or 90% lean)
  • 1 med onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 Tbsp ketchup
  • 1 Tbsp worcestershire
  • 3 Tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 3/4 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt or to taste
  • 1 1/2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp ground paprika

Sauce Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cup ketchup
  • 1 1/2 tsp white vinegar
  • 2 1/2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F
  2. Mix up all the ingredients (keeping the sauce separate). I just throw it all into a big bowl and use my hands, but I have heard of people using a large resealable plastic bag by putting in the ingredients, sealing it and kneading it. How you do it is up to you!
  3. Put meat mixture into a 9"x5" bread pan
  4. Bake for 40 minutes
  5. After 40 minutes has passed, take it out and spread the sauce out over the top
  6. Bake an additional 15-20 minutes or until internal temp reaches 160°F.
  7. Cool 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
